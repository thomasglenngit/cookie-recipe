Best Chocolate Chip Cookies

Everybody loves a good cookie! Try this recipe and share with friends!

This recipe is from my dad and they are a favorite among friends and family. The secret ingredient is the coconut! Be warned, these will fly off of the plate!

Baked biscuits have existed for all of recorded history. But, cookies as we know them today originated in medieval Persia. Even then, they were not as sweet as their modern counterparts. It wasn't until the 17th century that the Dutch brought what they called the "koekje" to New Amsterdam (modern day New York). From there, they became a staple of North American diet. Whenever I go to Europe, my friends want me to make chocolate chip cookies like we do here in the US. It isn't always easy because they usually have gas ovens and there's usually a change in altitude. But I can talk about that in another entry.

Ingredients

  • Butter
  • White sugar
  • Brown sugar
  • Eggs
  • Vanilla
  • Flour
  • Baking soda
  • Salt
  • Chocolate chips
  • Oatmeal
  • Coconut

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 325.
  2. Beat the butter, sugar, eggs and vanilla together until creamy.
  3. Mix together the flour, baking soda and salt in a separate bowl.
  4. Add flour mixture to butter mixture slowly.
  5. Stir in chocolate chips, oatmeal and coconut.
  6. Bake for 10 minutes or until golden brown.
